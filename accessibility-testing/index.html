Author: Paulina Cosma

About

Hello, my name is Paulina Cosma, and I am a MLIS student at the University of Denver, as well as a current volunteer at this library. This term, I am taking a Web Content course, and we are learning about the importance of digital accessibility and effective strategies to make web content more inclusive for people with disabilities, including those who use assistive technologies. We were asked to perform accessibility testing on a local organization’s website to identify potential issues that might prevent people with disabilities or those using assistive devices from accessing the information easily. I chose to test your website.


Did you know?
As of July 15, 2024, the CDC reported that more than one in four adults in the United States has a disability, representing 28.7% of the total population (CDC, 2024).


This report aims to help The Plainsboro Public Library better align with Section 508 and the Americans with Disabilities Act Title II Regulations. The accessibility checklist is based on the W3C Easy Checks. Assessment tools like WAVE and ANDI, along with keyboard testing, were used. I hope this report proves helpful. Thank you for reviewing my recommendations.

Accessibility Checklist

Page Titles: Needs Improvement

Page titles are important because they help all users navigate browser tabs when multiple webpages are open. More importantly, they are the first thing screen readers read to help users with disabilities in knowing where they are. The best way to make page titles accessible is to “front-load' critical or unique information first. Currently, your page titles read:

  • Book Club – Plainsboro Public Library
  • Visually Impaired Services offered thru the Talking Book & Braille…
  • Events & Bookings | Plainsboro Public Library
  • Enjoy the Art Gallery Exhibits Presented by the Plainsboro Public…

I recommend updating your titles to match the approach used with your Book Club Webpage:

  • Visually Impaired Services - Plainsboro Public Library
  • Events & Bookings – Plainsboro Public Library
  • Art Gallery – Plainsboro Public Library
